## **Classification Metrics (General ML)**

### **1. F1 Score**
**What it is:** Harmonic mean of precision and recall.
$$F_1 = 2 \cdot \frac{\text{Precision} \times \text{Recall}}{\text{Precision} + \text{Recall}}$$

**How scored:** Ranges 0–1; higher is better.
- **Good:** >0.40 on imbalanced trading data
- **Bad:** <0.20 (your script shows ~0.37, which is moderate given low base rate)

**Why it matters:** Balances the false-positive/false-negative tradeoff. Useful when both errors cost you.

---

### **2. Precision**
**What it is:** Of all trades predicted as winners, what fraction actually won?
$$\text{Precision} = \frac{TP}{TP + FP}$$

**How scored:** 0–1; higher is better.
- **Good:** >0.30 in FX (above random ~23% base rate)
- **Bad:** <0.23 (no edge over random guessing)

**Why it matters:** Directly tied to your gating decision. If precision is low, most approved trades lose. Your script shows **0.233 precision**, which is barely above the natural win rate (~23.64%), meaning the model adds minimal edge.

---

### **3. Recall**
**What it is:** Of all trades that actually won, what fraction did we catch?
$$\text{Recall} = \frac{TP}{TP + FN}$$

**How scored:** 0–1; higher is better.
- **Good:** >0.70 (catch most wins)
- **Bad:** <0.30 (miss most opportunities)

**Why it matters:** Determines missed upside. Your script shows **0.96 recall**, which means the model catches almost all winning trades—but at the cost of accepting many losers (hence low precision).

---

## **Probability Calibration Metrics**

### **4. PR-AUC (Precision-Recall Area Under Curve)**
**What it is:** Integral of precision across all possible recall thresholds.

$$\text{PR-AUC} = \int_0^1 \text{Precision}(r) \, dr$$

**How scored:** 0–1; higher is better.
- **Good:** >0.20 (especially for imbalanced data; baseline is class ratio)
- **Bad:** <0.10 (model is essentially random)
- **Random baseline:** ~0.236 (your natural win rate)

**Why it matters:** Unlike ROC-AUC, PR-AUC focuses on the minority class (winners) in imbalanced data. Your models show **0.24 PR-AUC**, which is *just barely above baseline*—a red flag that the model has almost no predictive power despite high recall.

---

### **5. Brier Score**
**What it is:** Mean squared error between predicted probability and actual outcome.
$$\text{Brier} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (\hat{p}_i - y_i)^2$$

**How scored:** 0–1; **lower is better** (unlike other metrics).
- **Good:** <0.20
- **Bad:** >0.25
- **Random predictor (50% confidence everywhere):** 0.25

**Why it matters:** Penalizes overconfident wrong predictions. Brier Score rewards both *correct predictions* and *accurate confidence*. Your score of **0.25** is at the "random predictor" ceiling—the model is essentially as well-calibrated as guessing 50/50.

## **Trading-Specific Metrics**

### **6. Turnover**
**What it is:** Fraction of total signals that you actually approve (threshold > decision boundary).
$$\text{Turnover} = \frac{\text{# Approved Trades}}{\text{Total Trades}}$$

**How scored:** 0–1; context-dependent.
- **Good:** 0.05–0.30 (selective, reduces costs)
- **Bad:** >0.90 (approves almost everything; high execution costs)

**Why it matters:** Transaction costs and slippage erode returns at high turnover. Your gatekeeper shows **0.96–1.0 turnover**, meaning it approves nearly every trade—defeating the purpose of a gating filter.

---

### **7. Expectancy UnitR**
**What it is:** Average return per trade, measured in units of risk (Risk:Reward ratio context).

$$\text{Expectancy}_{\text{UnitR}} = \mathbb{E}[\text{Return}] = \sum (\text{Win} \cdot 1.0) + (\text{Loss} \cdot -1.0)$$

**How scored:** Can be positive or negative.
- **Good:** >0.05 (positive expected value)
- **Bad:** <0 (negative edge; -0.53 in your script)

**Why it matters:** The bottom line. Your script shows **-0.53**, meaning on average approved trades lose ~0.53 units of whatever risk you take. This is a **critical failure sign**—the model predicts loss, not gain.

---

### **8. Threshold**
**What it is:** The decision boundary for converting probabilities (0–1) to trade/no-trade (0 or 1).

$$\text{Prediction} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } P(\text{win}) \geq \text{threshold} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

**How scored:** 0–1; depends on optimization objective.
- **0.5:** Default (unbiased)
- **0.45–0.50:** Conservative gating (fewer false positives)
- **0.20–0.30:** Aggressive gating (catch wins, accept losses)

**Why it matters:** Controls precision vs. recall tradeoff. Your script shows **threshold ≈ 0.20**, which explains the 96% recall and 23% precision—it approves any trade with >20% win probability, which is barely above random.
